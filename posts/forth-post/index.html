Supply chain on the block

My bachelor's thesis explored the real-world potential of blockchain technology in the context of supply chains. The goal was to go beyond theory and create a working proof-of-concept that shows how smart contracts can bring transparency, security, and automation to logistical operations.

The solution was implemented in Solidity and simulated using Ganache and Truffle. It introduces distinct roles - manufacturer, distributor, retailer - and lets each party interact with the supply chain through a permissioned system of smart contracts. These interactions are immutable, traceable, and enforce rules automatically.

My responsibilities included defining the system architecture, writing and deploying the smart contracts, testing via JavaScript, and linking the backend to a simulated front-end. The platform ensures that each product’s lifecycle - from creation to sale - is recorded and verifiable without third-party intermediaries.

This project deepened my understanding of decentralized systems and exposed me to both the power and limitations of emerging web3 infrastructure. It served as a sandbox to explore Ethereum’s ecosystem and tackle problems like data integrity, role-based permissions, and transparent automation.

In a world where trust is currency, blockchain has the potential to rewrite the rules of cooperation. This thesis was my small contribution to that vision.
